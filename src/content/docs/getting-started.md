LumberLinq is a Timber Management System designed for timber exporters, importers, traders, and logistics teams. It centralises tally sheets, shipments, inventory, business partners, and reporting in one place.

## Sign In

Open [app.lumberlinq.com](https://app.lumberlinq.com) in your browser and sign in with your company email and password. If you have not been invited yet, contact your company administrator.

## Your First Steps

| Step | What to do |
|------|-----------|
| 1 | Add your **Business Partners** — buyers, sellers, and trade parties |
| 2 | Add your **Products** — round log or square/sawn species |
| 3 | Add your **Loading Sites** — yards, mills, forests, and ports |
| 4 | Create a **Tally Sheet** to start measuring timber |
| 5 | Create a **Shipment** and link transport units to it |
| 6 | View **Reports** for operational and financial insights |

## Key Concepts

**Transport Unit (TU)** — A truck load, container, or batch of timber that is measured via a tally sheet and tracked through inventory and shipments.

**Tally Sheet** — The measurement record attached to a transport unit. Round tally sheets use Length and Girth; square tally sheets use Width, Thickness, Length, and Pieces.

**Shipment** — A logistics record that links one or more transport units with parties, routing, documents, and payments.

**Business Partner** — A buyer, seller, exporter, importer, or service party reused across tally sheets, shipments, and reports.
