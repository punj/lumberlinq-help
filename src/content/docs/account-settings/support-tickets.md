Support Tickets let you raise, track, and follow up on help requests from inside LumberLinq. Open at `/support/tickets`.

## Ticket List

![Support ticket list](/screenshots/support/support-01-ticket-list-rich.png)

The ticket list shows all support tickets raised by your company. Each row shows:

- Category (Tally Sheet, Shipments, Billing, Technical, etc.)
- Subject or issue summary
- Current status (Open, In Progress, Resolved, Closed)
- Date created and last updated

Use the list to track ongoing issues and check whether a previous ticket covers a current problem before opening a new one.

## Ticket Detail

![Support ticket detail](/screenshots/support/support-02-ticket-detail.png)

Open any ticket to see the full conversation: your original issue, replies from the support team, status updates, and ticket metadata. You can reply from this screen to provide additional information or follow up on an open ticket.

All admins and the user who created the ticket can view the ticket detail.

## Creating a New Ticket

![New ticket form](/screenshots/support/support-03-new-ticket-form.png)

Click **New Ticket** and fill in:

1. **Category** — select the module or area the issue relates to

   ![Category dropdown](/screenshots/support/support-04-new-ticket-category-dropdown.png)

2. **Subject** — a short, specific description of the problem
3. **Issue description** — explain what you were trying to do, what happened instead, and the exact error message if one appeared

## What to Include for Faster Resolution

The more specific the ticket, the faster the support team can locate and fix the issue:

- The exact module and action (e.g., "Adding a TU to Shipment #BL12345")
- Transport Unit ID, Shipment BL number, or tally sheet reference
- Product name or Business Partner name involved
- A screenshot of the error
- Your **Company ID** — find it in [Company Profile → Identity tab](/account-settings/company-profile/)

Vague descriptions like "it's not working" require multiple back-and-forth replies before the issue can be identified. A precise description with references resolves faster.
